Computer system design faces a multitude of challenges today, given the expectations of reliable high performance software, and low-power execution, on affordable hardware. RSSL is dedicated to the development of novel architectures and automation methodologies that support hardware-software co-design and optimization of heterogeneous multiprocessor systems. [Recent topics](research/) include research on: * Computer architecture and automated computer system design * Fault-tolerant and safety-critical system design * Aerospace and automative system security * Hardware-software optimization of machine learning systems RSSL is directed by [Professor Brett H. Meyer](people/), who has more than 15 years of experience in research on the optimization of computer systems; the lab is supported by funding from the [Natural Sciences and Engineering Research Council of Canada (NSERC)](https://www.nserc.crsng.gc.ca), the [Fonds de recherche du Québec (FRQNT)](https://frq.gouv.qc.ca), and, industrial sponsors.
